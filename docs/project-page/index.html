Abstract

Deformable manipulation policies often overfit to a small number of object states because collecting diverse demonstrations is expensive and rigid augmentation cannot capture topology-changing dynamics. DeformGen addresses this by synthesizing topology-diverse deformable states through physics-based perturbation and by transferring the source demonstration with deformation-aware trajectory warping.

The resulting pipeline produces replay-validated trajectories, exports policy-ready LeRobot data, and supports training and evaluation across rope routing, toy packing, and cloth folding tasks.

Method

State perturbation and trajectory synthesis are coupled through simulation replay.

1. Perturb and Stabilize

After initializing a source demonstration frame, DeformGen applies local runtime perturbations and simulates the object until a stable, physically valid state is reached.

2. Warp and Replay

Particle correspondences define a local deformation field that warps grasp poses and manipulation trajectories before replay validation.
